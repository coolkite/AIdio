Machine Learning

We implemented a total of three machine learning models: (1) capture and identify hand joints (training data set), 
(2) identify different American Sign Language words, and 
(3) process speech to text.

Hand Joints and Tensorflow
Early on in the project, we had trouble finding ways to identify ASL hand signs without needing time beyond 36-hours to train the model. Passing in videos, pictures, or anything pixelated as parameters to Tensorflow would take several hours to days to render. 

Our solution was to map hand joints, normalize the ratio of each joint to a certain point at the bottom of the hand between the range [0,-1], and pass in a CSV file of numbers. This way, we were able to train our model at a much faster rate (almost 60 fold more efficient) than if we used a Convulational Neural Network (CNN). We achieved a high accuracy level above 95% consistently.
