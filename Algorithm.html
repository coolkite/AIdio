Convolutional 
Neural Network (CNN)

  • Used by almost all ASL to text models
  • Takes a lot of time to process data due to large load (pixels, colors, etc.)
  • Very computationally intensive
  • High error and bias due to: dependence on good lighting conditions, hand color, high camera resolution

Kaggle's ASL data set does "cropping to hands-only, gray-scaling, resizing, and then creating at least 50+ variations to enlarge the quantity" (4+ steps).

AIdio

  • Uniquely tests data from identifying joints using object detection, exploring the specific ratio distances of joints
  • Very fast processing speed about 60x faster
  • Easy to process numeric data
  • Low error and bias: minimum contrast needed, absolutely no hand color dependence, low camera resolution possible 

>97% accuracy with 480p resolution camera. 
No additional post-editing required.

Hand Joints and Tensorflow

Early on in the project, we had trouble finding ways to identify ASL hand signs efficiently in less than HackUMass' designated 36 hours. Passing in pixelated videos and pictures as parameters to Tensorflow takes several hours to days to render and process because colors and exact coordinates are passed into the CNN model.

Our solution is to map hand joints, normalize the ratio of each joint to a certain point at the bottom of the hand between the range [0,-1], and pass in a CSV file of matrices (containing only floats). This way, we are able to train our model at a much faster rate and load the data within the bounds of one hour compared to the many hours a CNN would take. Our lighting in a moderatly lit classroom has almost no impact on our results. Likewise, due to the lack of parametized RGB values, there is no ethnic or racial bias in our model.
